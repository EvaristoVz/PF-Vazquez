MASTER OF PUPPETS (1986)

Personnel:
James Hetfield (Rhythmic Guitar, Vocals)
Kirk Hammett (Lead Guitar)
Cliff Burton (Bass)
Lars Ulrich (Drums)

"Master of Puppets," released on March 3, 1986, stands as Metallica's monumental third studio album, acclaimed for its intricate thrash metal brilliance. Notably, it was the last album to feature bassist Cliff Burton, tragically losing him in a tour bus accident later that year. The album's iconic title track, along with intense compositions like "Battery" and the melodic introspection of "Welcome Home (Sanitarium)," showcases Metallica's diverse musical range. With a powerful exploration of themes such as control, addiction, and psychological struggles, the album's lyrics delve deep into the human experience. The cover art, featuring tangled puppet strings, metaphorically captures its themes. A masterpiece of both sound and sentiment, "Master of Puppets" continues to be a lasting testament to Metallica's influence and legacy in the realm of heavy metal.
