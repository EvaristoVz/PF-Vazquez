RIDE THE LIGHTNING (1984)

Personnel:
James Hetfield (Rhythmic Guitar, Vocals)
Kirk Hammett (Lead Guitar)
Cliff Burton (Bass)
Lars Ulrich (Drums)

"Ride the Lightning," released on July 27, 1984, represents a significant leap forward for Metallica. It's the last album to feature Dave Mustaine, who would go on to form Megadeth, and the first to showcase Kirk Hammett's guitar talents. The album's opener, "Fight Fire with Fire," marks one of the band's fastest and most intense tracks. A remarkable aspect is the instrumental "The Call of Ktulu," highlighting Metallica's venture into more progressive territory. One of the most intriguing tracks, "Escape," was written under pressure from the record label. However, the band later expressed their dissatisfaction with it. The album's cover art, depicting a striking electric chair scene, reinforces its impactful title. "Ride the Lightning" captured the band's growing maturity and established them as trailblazers in the emerging thrash metal scene, setting the stage for their legendary future.
