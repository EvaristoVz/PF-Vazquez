Current Members

Metallica was founded around 1981 by Lars Ulrich and James Hetfield with the bassist Ron McGovney. Lars went to the United States with the idea of forming a band, so he started looking up for someone to do it with. James Hetfield responded to the advertisement and brought his friend, Ron McGovney, with him to play the bass. Soon After, Dave Mustaine (current leader of Megadeth) joined as the lead guitarrist and together they released a few demos.
After a few changes in the formation Cliff Burton and Kirk Hammett replaced McGovney and Mustaine, correspondingly.
This was the formation that, in 1983, released Kill 'Em All, their first album, and the next two.
